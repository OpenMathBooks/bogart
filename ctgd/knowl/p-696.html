Thus the truth of the distributive law for distributing an intersection over a union of \(n-1\) sets implies its truth for distributing an intersection over a union of \(n\) sets. Therefore by the principle of mathematical induction, the distributive law \(\displaystyle A\cap\bigcup_{i=1}^nS_i= \bigcup_{i=1}^nA\cap S_i\) holds for all positive integers \(n\text{.}\)
