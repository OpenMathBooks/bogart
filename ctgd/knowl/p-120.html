There are a number of different notations for the number of \(k\)-element permutations of an \(n\)-element set. The one we shall use was introduced by Don Knuth; namely \(n^{\underline{k}}\text{,}\) read “\(n\) to the \(k\) falling” or “\(n\) to the \(k\) down”. In Problem 20 you may have shown that

\begin{equation} n^{\underline{k}} =n(n-1)\cdots (n-k+1)= \prod_{i=1}^k( n-i+1).\tag{1.1} \end{equation}
