Note that \(\qchoose{n}{0}= \qchoose{n}{n} = 1\text{,}\) because only the partition with no parts sits in a rectangle of width or depth 0. But \(\frac{[m+0]!_q}{[m]!_q[0]!_q} =1\) and \(\frac{[0+n]!_q}{[0]!_q[n]!_q} =1\text{.}\) Thus the formula holds when \(m=0\) or \(n=0\text{.}\) But

\begin{align*} \amp q^m\frac{[m+n-1]!_q}{[n-1]!_q[m]!_q}+\frac{[m+n-1]!_q}{[n]!_q[m-1]!_q}\\ =\amp \relax[m+n-1]!_q\left(\frac{q^m}{[n-1]!_q[m]!_q} +\frac{1}{[n]!_q[m-1]!_q}\right)\\ \amp \relax[m+n-1]!_q\left(\frac{[n]_qq^m}{[n]!_q[m]_q}+\frac{[m]_q}{[n]!_q[m]!_q}\right)\\ =\amp \relax\frac{[m+n-1]!_q}{[n]!_q[m]!_q}\left((1+q+\cdots+q^{n-1})q^m + 1+q+\cdots+q^{m-1}\right)\\ =\amp \relax\frac{[m+n-1]!_q[m+n]_q}{[n]!_q[m]!_q}\\ =\amp \qchoose{m+n}{n}. \end{align*} in-context
