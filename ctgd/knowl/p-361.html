It is true, because if \(n>0\text{,}\) when you expand \((1-1)^n\) by the binomial theorem, you get an alternating sum of binomial coefficients equal to 0, and so the sum of the binomial coefficients \(\binom{n}{i}\) with \(i\) even must equal the sum of the binomial coefficients \(\binom{n}{i}\) with \(i\) odd.
