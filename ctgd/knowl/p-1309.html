Assuming \(c\not=1\) and using the method of partial fractions gives us

\begin{align*} \amp \frac{a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x}{(1-x)(1-cx)}\\ =\amp (a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x) \left[\frac{1/(1-c)}{(1-x)} -\frac{c/(1-c)}{1-cx}\right]\\ =\amp (a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x)\left[\frac{1}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty x^i-\frac{c}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty c^ix^i\right]\\ =\amp \frac{(a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x)}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty (1-c^{i+1})x^i. \end{align*} in-context
