(Note that \(mn\) is odd if and only if \(m+n\) is even and \(n\) is odd.) Wouldn't it be fascinating to know what we are counting here? It is the number of lattice paths from \((0, 0)\) to \((n - k, k)\) that are symmetric under 180 degree rotation. This and other results are discussed in the paper by John R. Stembridge “Some hidden relations involving the ten symmetry classes of plane partitions.” J. Combin. Theory Ser. A 68 (1994), no. 2, 372–409.
