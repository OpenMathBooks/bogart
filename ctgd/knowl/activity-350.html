Another relation that you may have learned about in school, perhaps in the guise of “clock arithmetic,” is the relation of equivalence modulo \(n\text{.}\) For integers (positive, negative, or zero) \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) we write \(a \equiv b \pmod{n}\) to mean that \(a-b\) is an integer multiple of \(n\text{,}\) and in this case, we say that \(a\) is congruent to \(b\) modulo \(n\) and write \(a\equiv b \pmod{n}\text{..}\) Show that the relation of congruence modulo \(n\) is an equivalence relation.
