We begin by computing a few values of \(a_n=\sum_{k=0}^n\binom{n-k}{k}\text{.}\) We have \(a_0=1\text{,}\) \(a_1=1\text{,}\) \(a_2=2\text{,}\) \(a_3=1+2=3\text{,}\) \(a_4=1+3+1=5\text{,}\) \(a_5=1+4+3=8\) and \(a_6=1+5+6+1=13\text{.}\) So far the sequence agrees with the Fibonacci Numbers. Each term of the sequence is the sum of the two preceding terms, so it makes sense to try to prove that \(a_n=a_{n-1}+a_{n-2}\text{.}\) We may write

\begin{align*} a_{n-1}+a_{n-2} =\amp \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \binom{n-1-k}{k} +\sum_{k=0}^{n-2} \binom{n-2-k}{k}\\ =\amp \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \binom{n-1-k}{k}+\sum_{j=1}^{n-1}\binom{n-1-j}{j-1}\\ =\amp 1+\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\binom{n-1-k}{k}+\binom{n-1-k}{k-1}\\ =\amp 1+\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\binom{n-k}{k} = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\binom{n-k}{k} = a_n \end{align*}
