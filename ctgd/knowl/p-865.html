How do we express powers of \(x\) in terms of powers of \(x+1\text{?}\) Some experimentation would help us guess how to do so; however there is a really nice trick that also isn't hard to see. Namely, we can write

\begin{align*} x^n =(x+1-1)^n= [(x+1) -1]^n =\amp \sum_{i=0}^n \binom{n}{i}(x+1)^{n-i}(-1)^i\\ =\amp \sum_{i=0}^n \binom{n}{i}(x+1)^i(-1)^{n-i} \end{align*}
