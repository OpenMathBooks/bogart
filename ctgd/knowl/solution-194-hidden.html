If \(n=0\) we get \((ab)^0=1\) and \(a^0b^0=1\text{.}\) Assume inductively that \((ab)^{n-1}=a^{n-1}b^{n-1}\text{.}\) Then by the inductive definition, inductive hypothesis, and commutative law,

\begin{equation*} (ab)^n= (ab)^{n-1}ab=a^{n-1}b^{n-1}ab=a^{n-1}ab^{n-1}b=a^nb^n. \end{equation*}

Thus the fact that \((ab)^{n-1}=a^{n-1}b^{n-1}\) implies the fact that \((ab)^n= a^nb^n\text{.}\) Therefore by the principle of mathematical induction, \((ab)^n=a^nb^n\) for all nonnegative integers \(n\text{.}\)
