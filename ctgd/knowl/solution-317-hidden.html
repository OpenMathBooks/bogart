\((1+x+x^2+x^3+x^4)^3\text{.}\) We can write

\begin{align*} \amp (1+x+x^2+x^3+x^4)^3 \rangle =\rangle \left(\frac{1-x^5}{1-x}\right)^3\\ =\amp (1-x^5)^3(1-x)^{-3}\\ =\amp (1-3x^5+3x^{10}-x^{15})\sum_{i=0}^\infty \binom{3+i-1}{i}x^i\\ =\amp (1-3x^5+3x^{10}-x^{15})\sum_{i=0}^\infty \binom{2+i}{i}x^i \end{align*}

The coefficient of \(x^{10}\) is the number of ways to pass out ten pieces of candy, and is \(\binom{12}{10}-3\binom{7}{5} +3\binom{2}{0}\text{.}\) Thus the number of ways to pass out ten pieces of candy is \(66-3\cdot21+3=6\text{.}\))
