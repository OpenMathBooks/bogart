Solution 319.1

We effectively answered this question in our solution to Problem 315. In particular, there are three rotations of 180 degrees through the centers of opposite faces. The other kinds of group elements are as follows.

  • the 90 and 270 degree rotations around an axis through two faces, of which we have six. Their cycle structure consists of a four-cycle and two one-cycles.

  • the 120 and 240 degree rotations around an axis connecting two diagonally opposite vertices. Their cycle structure consists of two three-cycles. We have eight of these.

  • the 180 degree rotations around an axis connecting two opposite edges; their cycle structure consists of three two-cycles. We have six of these.

  • the identity, whose cycle structure is six one-cycles.

As we said in the solution to Problem 318 ``The cycle structure determines the factored enumerator; a cycle of size \(i\) gives a factor of \((R^i+B^i)\text{.}\) That is because a coloring fixed by a group element has to be constant on the cycles of that group element. If a cycle has size \(i\text{,}\) it contributes a summand of \(P^i\) to the picture enumerator for colorings of that cycle for each picture \(P\) of a possible value of the function. Problem 313 tells us to multiply these individual picture enumerators together.''
