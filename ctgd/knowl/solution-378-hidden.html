\(a_n= a_{n-1} +c(a_{n-1}-a_{n-2})=(1+c)a_{n-1}-ca_{n-2}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=2}^\infty a_nx^n =\amp \sum_{n=2}^\infty(1+c)a_{n-1}x^n-c\sum_{n=2}^\infty a_{n-2}x^n\\ (1-(1+c)x +cx^2)\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n =\amp a_0+a_1x-a_0(1+c)x\\ \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n =\amp \frac{a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x}{1-(1+c)x +cx^2}\\ =\amp \frac{a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x}{(1-x)(1-cx)} \end{align*}

Assuming \(c\not=1\) and using the method of partial fractions gives us

\begin{align*} \amp \frac{a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x}{(1-x)(1-cx)}\\ =\amp (a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x) \left[\frac{1/(1-c)}{(1-x)} -\frac{c/(1-c)}{1-cx}\right]\\ =\amp (a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x)\left[\frac{1}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty x^i-\frac{c}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty c^ix^i\right]\\ =\amp \frac{(a_0+(a_1-a_0(1+c))x)}{1-c}\sum_{i=0}^\infty (1-c^{i+1})x^i. \end{align*}

From this we get that

\begin{equation*} a_i=\frac{a_0}{1-c}(1-c^{i+1}) +\frac{a_1-a_0(1+c)}{1-c}(1-c^i). \end{equation*}

Assuming that we begin with one meter of new wood means \(a_0=1\text{,}\) and assuming we have a total of two meters of new wood at the end of the first year means \(c=2\) and \(a_1=3\text{.}\) Substituting these into our formula for \(a_i\) gives us \(a_i=2^{i+1}-1\)
