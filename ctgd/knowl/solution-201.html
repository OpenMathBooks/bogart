Solution2.4.8.1

When \(n=1\) we get \(\displaystyle\left(\prod_{i=1}^1 a_i\right)^k=a_i^k =\prod_{i=1}^1a_i^k\text{.}\) Now assume inductively that \(\displaystyle\left(\prod_{i=1}^{m-1}a_i\right)^k=\prod_{i=1}^{m-1}a_i^k\text{.}\) Then we may write

\begin{equation*} \left(\prod_{i=1}^ma_i\right)^k=\left(\left(\prod_{i=1}^{m-1} a_i\right)\cdot a_m\right)^k=\left(\prod_{i=1}^{m-1} a_i^k\right)\cdot a_m^k=\prod_{i=1}^m a_i^k. \end{equation*}

Thus the correctness of the formula for \(n=m-1\) implies its correctness for \(n=m\text{.}\) Therefore by the principle of mathematical induction, the formula holds for all positive integers \(n\text{.}\)
