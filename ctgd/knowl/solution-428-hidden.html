Since \((h\circ(g\circ f)) (x) = h((g\circ f)(x)) = h(g(f(x)))\) and \(((h\circ g)\circ f) (x) = (h\circ g)(f(x)) = h(g(f(x)))\text{,}\) we have that

\begin{equation*} h\circ (g\circ f) = (h\circ g)\circ f. \end{equation*}

This says that the associative law holds for the composition operation in a group of permutations.
