Composition of the elements of a permutation group on the left by \(\sigma\) permutes the elements of the group, so \(\overline{\sigma}\) is a permutation of \(G\text{.}\) The size of an orbit is the size of the subgroup, because if \(\sigma\circ\tau_1 = \sigma\circ\tau_2\text{,}\) then \(\tau_1 = \tau_2\text{.}\) Since \(G\) is the union of the orbits of \(H\) and these orbits all have the same size, by the quotient principle the size of \(H\) divides the size of \(G\text{.}\)
