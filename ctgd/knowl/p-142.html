A function from \(X\) to \(Y\) is one-to-one and onto if, in its digraph, exactly one arrow goes into each vertex representing a member of \(Y \text{.}\) (For a digraph to be the digraph of a function from \(X\) to \(Y\text{,}\) one and only one arrow must come out of each vertex representing a member of \(X\text{.}\))
