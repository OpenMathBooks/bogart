From the previous part of this problem, we will make a total of \(\frac{m-n}{2} + \frac{m+n}{2} = m\) steps, and our total motion parallel to the \(y\) axis will be \(\frac{m+n}{2}-\frac{m-n}{2} = n\text{.}\) The length of such a path is \(m\sqrt{2}\text{;}\) we might informally just call it \(m\) steps.
