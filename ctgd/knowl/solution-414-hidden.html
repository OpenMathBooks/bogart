If we use \(C_n\) to stand for a cycle on \(n\) vertices and \(P_n\) to stand for a path on \(n\) vertices, then by the deletion-contraction recurrence, we may write

\begin{align*} \hspace{-.4 in}\chi_{C_n}(x) \amp = \chi_{P_n}(x)-\chi_{C_{n-1}}(x)\\ \amp = \chi_{P_n}(x)-\chi_{P_{n-1}}(x)+\chi_{C_{n-2}}(x)\\ \amp = \chi_{P_n}(x)-\chi_{P_{n-1}}(x)+\chi_{P_{n-3}}(x)- \cdots+(-1)^{n-3}(\chi_{P_3}(x)-\chi_{C_2}(x))\\ \amp = x(x-1)^{n-1}-x(x-1)^{n-2}+x(x-1)^{n-3}\cdots\\ \amp \quad + (-1)^{n-3}[x(x-1)^2-x(x-1)]\\ \amp = x(x-1)\sum_{i=0}^{n-2}(x-1)^i(-1)^{n-2-i}\\ \amp = x(x-1)(-1)^{n-2}\sum_{i=0}^{n-2}(1-x)^i\\ \amp = x(x-1)(-1)^{n-2}\frac{1-(1-x)^{n-1}}{1-(1-x)}\\ \amp = (x-1)[(x-1)^{n-1}+(-1)^n]. \end{align*}

Here the dependence on \(n\) is interesting; effectively, we are taking \((x-1)\) times the result of dropping the constant term from \((x-1)^{n-1}\text{.}\)
