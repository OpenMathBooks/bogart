The generating function for the number of ways to paint \(n\) chairs is

\begin{align*} \amp \hspace{-.5in}(1+x+x^2+\cdots)^2(1+x)(1+x+x^2+x^3)(1+x^2+x^4+\cdots) (1+x^4+x^8+\cdots)\\ =\amp \frac{(1+x)(1+x+x^2+x^3)}{(1-x)^2(1-x^2)(1-x^4)}\rangle =\rangle \frac{1}{(1-x)^4} \end{align*}

Thus the number of ways to paint \(n\) chairs is \(\binom{n+4-1}{n}=\binom{n+3}{n}\)
