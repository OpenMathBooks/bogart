Solution 210.e.ii.1

We either have exactly \(k\) parts or fewer than \(k\) parts. In the first case, removing one from each part gives us a partition whose Young diagram fits into a \(m-1\) by \(n\) box, while in the second case doing nothing gives us a partition that fits into a \(m\) by \(n-1\) box. In the first case the partition we get partitions \(k-n\text{,}\) and in the second case it still partitions \(k\text{.}\) Thus we get

\begin{equation*} \qchoose{m+n}{n}= q^n\qchoose{m+n-1}{n}+\qchoose{m+n-1}{n-1}. \end{equation*}
