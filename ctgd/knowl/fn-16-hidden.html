For those interested in logic and set theory, given a family of subsets \(A_i\) of a set \(A\text{,}\) we define \(\bigcap_{i:i\in S}A_i\) to be the set of all members \(x\) of \(A\) that are in \(A_i\) for all \(i \in S\text{.}\) (Note that this allows \(x\) to be in some other \(A_j\)s as well.) Then if \(S = \emptyset\text{,}\) our intersection consists of all members \(x\) of \(A\) that satisfy the statement “if \(i\in \emptyset\text{,}\) then \(x \in A_i\text{.}\)” But since the hypothesis of the “if-then” statement is false, the statement itself is true for all \(x \in A\text{.}\) Therefor \(\bigcap_{i:i \in \emptyset}A_i = A\text{.}\)
