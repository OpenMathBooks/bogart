To color the vertices of a path, start at one end. There are \(x\) colors for that vertex, and \(x-1\) colors for each of the next \(n-1\text{,}\) since each of them must be different from the preceding one. Thus the chromatic polynomial of a path on \(n\) vertices is \(x(x-1)^{n-1}\text{.}\) The dependence on the number of vertices appears in the exponent on \(x-1\text{.}\)
