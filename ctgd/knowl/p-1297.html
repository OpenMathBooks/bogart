The number \(\binom{n}{k}\) is the number in row \(n\) and column \(k\) of the Pascal (right) triangle. We have said we want to have \(\binom{-n}{0}=\binom{n+0-1}{0}\text{,}\) so we want ones everywhere in that column. Now the Pascal recurrence gives us that \(\binom{-n}{0}=\binom{-n-1}{-1} +\binom{-n-1}{0}\text{,}\) so that \(\binom{-n}{-1}=0\text{,}\) as does \(\binom{0}{-1}\text{.}\) Applying the Pascal recurrence again gives us \(\binom{-n}{-1}= \binom{-n-1}{-2} +\binom{-n-1}{-1}\text{,}\) so we have \(\binom{-n-1}{-2}=0\) as well. Following this pattern, we can prove by induction that \(\binom{-n}{-k}\) is zero whenever \(k\) and \(n\) are positive.
