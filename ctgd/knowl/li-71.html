Item 3.14.a

We first solve the ordered degree sequence problem in which we assume \(d_i\) is the degree of vertex \(i\text{.}\) The number of times \(i\) appears in the Prüfer code of a tree is one less than the degree of \(i\text{,}\) so vertex \(i\) appears \(d_i - 1\) times. Thus the sum of the \(d_i -1\) should be \(2n-2-n = n-2\text{.}\) Of the \(n-2\) places in the Prüfer code, we want to label \(d_1 -1\) of them with 1, \(d_2 -1\) of them with 2 and in general \(d_i - 1\) of them with \(i\text{.}\) There are

\begin{equation*} \binom{n-2}{d_1-1,d_2-1,d_3-1,\dots,d_n-1} \end{equation*}

ways to do this, so the number of trees in which vertex \(i\) has degree \(d_i\) is \(\frac{(n-2)!}{(d_1-1)!(d_2-1)!\cdots(d_n-1)!}\text{.}\)
