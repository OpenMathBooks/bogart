Let \(T\) be the set of size less than \(n\text{,}\) and \(S\) be the set of size \(n\text{.}\) Let \(B_j=\{i|f(i)=j\}\) for each \(j\) in \(T\text{.}\) Then the nonempty sets among the \(B_j\)s form a partition of \(S\) and the number of blocks is less than the size of \(S\text{.}\) Therefore by the pigeonhole principle, there is at least one block with at least two elements, so there are two elements \(i_1\) and \(i_2\) such that \(f(i_1)=f(i_2)\text{.}\)
