Solution 369.2

For a noninductive proof, note that all factors of \(n\) are at least 2. If \(n\) is a power of two, then the number of times \(2\) is a factor of \(n\) is exactly \(\log_2 n\text{.}\) But if \(n\) is not a power of 2, we still have that \(2^{\log_2 n}=n\text{,}\) so the product of \(\lceil\log_2 n\rceil\) numbers including some greater than 2 must be greater than \(n\text{.}\) Therefore, the number of prime factors of \(n\) is no more than \(\log_2 n\text{.}\) Thus for all \(n\ge2\text{,}\)the number of prime factors of \(n\) must be less than or equal to \(\log_2 n\text{.}\)
