On two vertices: \(x_1x_2\text{.}\) On three vertices: \(x_1x_2x_3(x_1 + x_2 + x_3)\text{.}\) On three vertices: \(x_1x_2x_3x_4(x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + x_4)^2\text{.}\) We now explain the third answer. A tree on four vertices either has a vertex of degree three, or it doesn't. If vertex \(i\) has degree three, then the picture of the tree is \(x_1x_2x_3x_4x_i^2\) . If the tree has no vertex of degree 3, it has two vertices of degree 2. If \(i\) and \(j\) are the vertices of degree 2, then the picture of the tree is \(x_1x_2x_3x_4x_ix_j\text{.}\) Adding up all these pictures gives \(x_1x_2x_3x_4(x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + x_4)^2\text{.}\)
