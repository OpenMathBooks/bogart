When \(n=0\text{,}\) \(0=0(0+1)/2\text{,}\) so our formula holds. Now suppose that\(k>0\) and that our formula holds when \(n=k-1\text{,}\) so that \(1+2+\cdots+k-1=(k-1)k/2\text{.}\) Add \(k\) to both sides of this equation to get

\begin{align*} 1+2+\cdots+(k-1)+k =\amp (k-1)k/2 +k\\ =\amp k^2/2-k/2+k\\ =\amp k^2/2+k/2\\ =\amp k(k+1)/2. \end{align*} in-context
