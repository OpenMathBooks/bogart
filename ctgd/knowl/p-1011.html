If we take the complement of the Young diagram of a partition of \(k\) into \(n\) parts of size at most \(m\) in an rectangle with \(n\) rows and \(m\) columns, the number we partition will be \(mn-k\text{,}\) and we will have no more than \(n\) parts, each of size at most \(m-1\text{.}\) And if we take the complement of a partition of this second kind in the same rectangle, we will get a partition of the first kind.
