With one color of paint, there would have been one way to paint each pole so our EGF would be \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!}\text{,}\) or \(e^x\text{.}\) With two colors of paint, it would be \(e^{2x}\) by analogy with the solution to ProblemÂ 373. Thus the EGF for two colors of paint would be the square of the EGF for one color of paint. The EGF for four colors of paint is the fourth power of the EGF for one color of paint and the square of the EGF for two colors of paint.
