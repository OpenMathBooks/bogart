The generating function for ordered pairs of total value \(k\) will have the number of ordered pairs of total value \(k\) as the coefficient of \(x^k\text{.}\) But we get a total value \(k\) by taking something of value \(i\) in \(S_1\) and something of value \(k-i\) in \(j\text{.}\) And since values cannot be negative, the only \(i\)s available to us are the ones between \(0\) and \(k\text{.}\) By the product principle for pairs, the number of ordered pairs \((x,y)\) with \(v_1(x)=i\) and \(v_2(y)=k-i\) is \(a_ib_{k-i}\text{.}\) To get the number of pairs of total value \(k\text{,}\) we have to sum over all possible pairs \((i,k-i)\) of values, that is, we have to take the sum \(\sum_{i=0}^k a_ib_{k-i}\text{.}\) And this is the coefficient of \(x^k\) in the product

\begin{equation*} \left(\sum_{i=0}^\infty a_ix^i\right)\left(\sum_{j=0}^\infty b_jx^j\right). \end{equation*}

This proves the product principle for generating functions.
