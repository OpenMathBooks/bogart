We use induction on \(n\) to prove this. When \(n=0\text{,}\) the formula gives us \(a^{m+0} =a^ma^0=a^m\cdot 1=a^m\text{,}\) so the rule of exponents holds when \(n=0\text{.}\) Now assume it holds when \(n=k-1\) so that \(a^{m+k-1}=a^ma^{k-1}\text{.}\) Then, starting and ending with our inductive definition, we may write

\begin{equation*} a^{m+n}=aa^{m+n-1}=aa^ma^{k-1}=a^m\cdot a\cdot a^{k-1}=a^ma^k. \end{equation*}
