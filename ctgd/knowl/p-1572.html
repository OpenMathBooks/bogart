Thus it must be the case that \(c(k,n)= |s(k,n)|\text{.}\) To prove this, note that it is the case when \(k=0\text{,}\) and also when \(n=1\text{.}\) Now assume inductively it is true when \(k= m-1\) and notice that if \(n>0\text{,}\) \(s(m,n)=s(m-1,n-1)-(m-1)s(m-1,n)\text{,}\) that \(s(m-1,n-1)\) and \(s(m-1,n)\) have opposite signs, so
