If \(mk=hn\text{,}\) then \(hn=mk\text{,}\) so if \((m,n)\) is related to \((h,k)\text{,}\) then \((h,k)\) is related to \((m,n)\text{.}\) If \((m,n)\) is related to \((h,k)\) and \((h,k)\) is related to \((p,q)\text{,}\) then \(mk=hn\) and \(hq=pk\text{,}\) which gives us \(mkhq=hnpk\text{,}\) and cancelling \(h\) and \(k\) gives us \(mq=np=pn\text{,}\) so \((m,n)\) is related to \((p,q)\text{.}\) Therefore, the relation is transitive. This is the relation of equality of the fractions \(\frac{m}{n}\) and \(\frac{h}{k}\text{.}\)
