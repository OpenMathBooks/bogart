There are several ways of finding this relationship, but the most concise way is to observe that \((-x)^{\underline{k}}=(-1)^kx^{\overline{k}}\) and \((-x)^{\overline{k}}= (-1)^k x^{\underline{k}}\text{.}\) This lets us write

\begin{align*} (-x)^{\overline{k}} =\amp \sum_{n=0}^k b(k,n)(-x)^{\underline{n}}\\ (-1)^kx^{\underline{k}} =\amp \sum_{n=0}^k(-1)^nb(k,n)x^{\overline{n}}\\ x^{\underline{k}} =\amp \sum_{n=0}^k (-1)^{n-k}b(k,n)x^{\overline{n}}. \end{align*}

Therefore the change of basis coefficients are \((-1)^{n-k}b(k,n)\text{.}\)
