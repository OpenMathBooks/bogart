Let \(E\text{,}\) for enumerator, be the sum of all the pictures of all the elements in the union of the multiorbits of \(G\) acting on a set \(S\text{.}\) Recall that for any multiorbit \(M\) the picture \(P(M)\) is the picture \(P(x)\) of any element \(x\) of \(M\text{,}\) and the number of elements of a multiorbit \(M\) is always the size of \(G\text{.}\) This lets us write

\begin{align*} E &= \sum_{M\colon M\text{ is a multiorbit of }G}\sum_{x\colon x\in M} P(x)\\ &= \sum_{M\colon M\text{ is a multiorbit of }G}|G|P(M)\\ &= |G|\sum_{M\colon M\text{ is a multiorbit of }G}P(M)\\ &= |G|\Orb(G,S)\text{.} \end{align*}

Recall also that the multiplicity of an element \(x\) in its multiorbit, and thus in the union of the multiorbits, is \(|\Fix(x)|\text{.}\) This lets us write \(E = \sum_{x\colon x\in S}|\Fix(x)|P(x)\text{.}\) Now we have two possible approaches. First, the sum we just gave for \(E\) is the sum, over all ordered pairs \((\sigma,x)\) such that \(\sigma\) fixes \(x\text{,}\) of \(P(x)\text{.}\) But this is exactly \(\Fix(G,S)\text{.}\) Second, we can get the same formula for \(E\) by using \(\chi(\sigma,x)\) as in ProblemÂ 300. We may write

\begin{align*} E&= \sum_{x\colon x\in S} |\Fix(x)|P(x)\\ &=\sum_{x\colon x\in S}\sum_{\sigma\colon \sigma\in G}\chi(\sigma,x)P(x)\\ &= \sum_{\sigma\colon \sigma\in G}\sum_{x\colon \sigma x=x} P(x)\\ &=\Fix(G,S)\text{.} \end{align*}

Setting our two values of \(E\) equal and solving for \(\Orb(G,S)\) gives us

\begin{equation*} \Orb(G,S) = \frac{1}{|G|}\Fix(G,S)\text{.} \end{equation*}
