Problem44

We first gave this problem as Problem 12.a. Now we have several ways to approach the problem. A tennis club has \(2n\) members. We want to pair up the members by twos for singles matches.

(a)

In how many ways may we pair up all the members of the club? Give at least two solutions different from the one you gave in Problem 12.a. (You may not have done Problem 12.a. In that case, see if you can find three solutions.)

Hint
(b)

Suppose that in addition to specifying who plays whom, for each pairing we say who serves first. Now in how many ways may we specify our pairs? Try to find as many solutions as you can.
