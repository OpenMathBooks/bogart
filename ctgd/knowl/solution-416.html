Choose an edge \(e\) of \(G\text{.}\) Then \(\chi_G(x) = \chi_{G-e}(x)-\chi_{G/e}(x)\text{.}\) In \(G-e\text{,}\) we may assume inductively that \((-1)^{n-i}c'_i\ge0\) and in \(G-/e\) we can assume inductively that \(c''_i(-1)^{n-1-i}\ge0\text{,}\) where we use \(c'_i\) and \(c''_i\) as the coefficient of \(x^i\) in \(\chi_{G-e}(x)\) and \(\chi_{G/e}(x)\text{,}\) respectively. Then \(c_i=c'_i -c''_i\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} c_i(-1)^{n-i}=c'_i(-1)^{n-i}-c''_i(-1)^{n-i}=c'_i(-1)^{n-i}+c''_i(-1)^{n-1-i} \ge0. \end{equation*}

Therefore by the principle of mathematical induction, \(c_i(-1)^i\ge0\) for all finite graphs.
