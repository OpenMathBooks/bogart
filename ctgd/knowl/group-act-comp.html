Yes, because the action gives a permutation of the functions and \(\overline{\sigma\tau}(f) = f\circ(\sigma\circ)^{-1} = f\circ\tau^{-1}\circ\sigma^{-1}\text{,}\) while \(\overline{\sigma}\circ\overline{\tau}(f) = f\circ\tau^{-1}\circ\sigma^{-1}\text{.}\) Thus this is a group action.
