Solution88.1

To specify a bijection \(f\) from an \(n\)-element set \(\{a_1,a_2, \ldots a_n\}\) to an \(n\)-element set, we have \(n\) choices for \(f(a_1)\text{,}\) and then \(b_{n-1}\) choices for how to define \(f\) from the elements \(\{a_2,a_3, \ldots,a_n\}\) to the remaining \(n-1\) elements of our range. By the product principle this gives us \(b_n=nb_{n-1}\) for the number \(b_n\) of bijections from an \(n\)-element set to an \(n\)-element set.
