\(\frac{10}{(1-x-2x^2)}=\frac{10}{(1-2x)(1+x)} = \frac{c}{1-2x} +\frac{d}{1+x}\text{.}\) This gives us the equations \(c+d=10\) and \(c-2d=0\text{.}\) Thus \(3d=10\text{,}\) so \(d=\frac{10}{3}\text{,}\) and \(c=2d\) so \(c=\frac{20}{3}\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=0}^\infty a_ix^i \amp= \frac{10}{1-x-2x^2}\\ \amp= \frac{20/3}{1-2x} + \frac{10/3}{1+x}\\ \amp= \frac{20}{3}\sum_{i=0}^\infty (2x)^i + \frac{10}{3}\sum_{i=0}^\infty (-1)^ix^i\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus \(a_i=\frac{20}{3}2^i +\frac{10}{3}(-1)^i\text{.}\)
