\(\{\rho^2,\!\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\varphi_{1|3},\!\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\varphi_{2|4},\!\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\varphi_{12|34},\!\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\varphi_{14|23},\!\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\iota,\rho,\rho^2,\rho^3\}\text{,}\) \(\{\iota\}\text{,}\) \(\{\iota,\varphi_{1|3},\rho^2,\varphi_{2|4}\}\text{,}\) \(\{\iota,\varphi_{12|34},\rho^2,\varphi_{12|34}\}\text{,}\) and all of \(D_4\text{.}\) Notice that once a subgroup has \(\rho\) or \(\rho^3\) in it, it has all powers of \(\rho\text{,}\) and if it has all powers of \(\rho\) and just one flip, it has all the flips in it. But the product of two “different kinds” of flips is \(\rho\) or \(\rho^3\text{,}\) and this shows that our list of subgroups is complete.
