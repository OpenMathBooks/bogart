Let \(f(X) = X-\{n\}\text{,}\) the set \(X\) with \(n\) removed. This is a bijection because two different sets containing \(n\) must yield different sets when \(n\) is removed (one-to-one), and each \((k-1)\)-element subset \(X\) of \([n-1]\) may be obtained from the \(k\)-element subset \(X\cup \{n\}\) of \([n]\) by removing \(n\) (onto).
