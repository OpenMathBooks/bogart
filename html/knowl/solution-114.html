Solution64.1

If we take 40 powers of such a prime, either one will end in “01” or some two, say \(p^i\) and \(p^j\) with \(i>j\) must have the same last two digits by the pigeon hole principle. Then \(p^i-p^j=100k\) for some integer \(k\text{.}\) Thus \(p^j(p^{i-j} -1)\) must be a multiple of 100, and since neither 2 nor 5 divide \(p\text{,}\) \(p^{i-j} -1 = 100k'\) for some integer \(k'\) then \(p^{i-j} = 100k'+1\text{,}\) so the last two digits of \(p^{i-j}\) must be “01.”
