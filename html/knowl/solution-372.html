Solution4.4.1.1

\((x^3+x^4+x^5+x^6)^n\text{;}\)

\begin{align*} (x^3+x^4+x^5+x^6)^n =\amp x^{3n}(1+x+x^2+x^3)^n\\ =\amp x^{3n}\left(\frac{1-x^4}{1-x}\right)^n\\ =\amp x^{3n}\sum_{j=0}^n (-1)^j\binom{n}{j}x^{4j}\sum_{i=0}^\infty \binom{n+i-1}{i}x^i\text{.} \end{align*}

The number of ways to pass out \(k\) pieces of candy is the coefficient of \(x^k\) in this expression. Thus the answer is zero if \(k\lt 3n\) because of the \(x^{3n}\) in front. Otherwise the answer is the coefficient of \(^{k-3n}\) in \(\sum_{j=0}^n (-1)^j\binom{n}{j}x^{4j}\sum_{i=0}^\infty \binom{n+i-1}{i}x^i\text{,}\) which is

\begin{equation*} \sum_{i,j:4j+i=k-3n}(-1)^j\binom{n}{j}\binom{n+i-1}{i}= \sum_{j=0}^{\lfloor (k-3n)/4\rfloor} (-1)^j\binom{n}{j}\binom{k-2n-4j-1}{n-1}. \end{equation*} in-context
