A rotation through 60 or 300 degrees is a five-cycle; a rotation through 120 or 240 degrees is a product of two three-cycles. The cycle structure determines the factored enumerator; a cycle of size \(i\) gives a factor of \((R^i+B^i)\text{.}\) That is because a coloring fixed by a group element has to be constant on the cycles of that group element. If a cycle has size \(i\text{,}\) it contributes a summand of \(P^i\) to the picture enumerator for colorings of that cycle for each picture \(P\) of a possible value of the function.
