\(T(x) = \sum_{i=0}^\infty (x+x^2/2)^i/i!\text{.}\) By the binomial theorem, this gives \(T(x) = \sum_{i=0}^\infty \frac{\sum_{j=0}^i \binom{i}{j}x^j\bigl(\frac{x^2}{2}\bigr)^{i-j}}{ i!}=\sum_{i=0}^\infty\frac{\sum_{j=0}^i\binom{i}{j}x^{2i-j}2^{j-i}}{i!}\text{.}\) Then the coefficient \(c_n\) of \(x^n\) is the sum over all \(i\) and \(j\) with \(2i-j=n\) and \(j\le i\) of \(\binom{i}{j}\frac{n!}{i!}2^{j-i}\text{.}\) But if \(2i-j=n\text{,}\) then \(j= 2i-n\text{,}\) and if \(2i-n\le i\text{,}\) then \(i\le n\text{,}\) so that \(c_n = \frac{n!}{2^n}\sum_{i=0}^n\binom{i}{2i-n}\frac{2^i}{i!}\text{.}\) Note that \(\binom{i}{2i-n}\) is the same as \(\binom{i}{n-i}\text{,}\) which is 0 unless \(i\ge n/2\text{,}\) which reduces our sum to \(c_n = \frac{n!}{2^n}\sum_{i=\lceil n/2\rceil}^n\binom{i}{n-i}\frac{2^i}{i!}\text{.}\)
