Solution168.e.1

For the two partitions to have the same number of parts, either \(m'=m\) or \(n'=n\text{.}\) If \(m'=m\) and they have the same largest part, then \(n'>n\text{.}\) But this is consistent with \(n'-n\) being the multiplicity of the largest part of \(\lambda\text{.}\) Thus they can have the same number of parts and the same largest part if \(m'=m\) and \(n'-n\) is the multiplicity of the largest part of \(\lambda\text{,}\) or similarly if \(n=n'\) and \(m'-m\) is the smallest part of \(\lambda\text{.}\)
