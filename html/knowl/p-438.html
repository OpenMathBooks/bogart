Thus the truth of our formula for \(n=k-1\) implies its truth for \(n=k\text{.}\) Therefore by the principle of mathematical induction, our formula is true for all integers \(n\ge 2\text{.}\) We have already seen is is true when \(i=0\) or \(i=1\text{,}\) so it is true for all nonnegative \(n\) and all numbers \(i\) with \(0\le i\le n\text{.}\)
