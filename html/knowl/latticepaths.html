Problem48

Problem 47 has a geometric interpretation in a coordinate plane. A lattice path in the plane is a “curve” made up of line segments that either go from a point \((i,j)\) to the point \((i+1,j)\) or from a point \((i,j)\) to the point \((i,j+1)\text{,}\) where \(i\) and \(j\) are integers. (Thus lattice paths always move either up or to the right.) The length of the path is the number of such line segments.

(a)

What is the length of a lattice path from \((0,0)\) to \((m,n)\text{?}\)

(b)

How many such lattice paths of that length are there?

Hint

In Problem 47 you saw that we had to make ten choices of north or east, choosing north four times.

(c)

How many lattice paths are there from \((i,j)\) to \((m,n)\text{,}\) assuming \(i\text{,}\) \(j\text{,}\) \(m\text{,}\) and \(n\) are integers?

Hint

This problem is actually a bit tricky. What happens to the answer if \(i \gt m\) or \(j \gt n\text{?}\) Remember that paths go up or to the right.
