Item6.4.7.c

The action proposed in partÂ 6.4.7.b is the same as our action on colorings. To see why, if

\begin{align*} &\{(1,g(1)),(2,g(2)),\dots,(n,g(n))\}\\ =&\{(\sigma(1),f(1)),(\sigma(2),f(2)),\dots,(\sigma(n),f(n))\}\text{,} \end{align*}

and \(i=\sigma(j)\text{,}\) then \(j=\sigma^{-1}(i)\) and so

\begin{equation*} (i,g(i)) = (\sigma(j),f(j)) = (\sigma(\sigma^{-1}(i)),f(\sigma^{-1}(i)) = (i,f(\sigma^{-1}(i))\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore, \(g=f\circ \sigma^{-1}\text{.}\)
