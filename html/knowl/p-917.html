If \(m'>m\) and \(n'=n\text{.}\) the two partitions will have the same number of parts, because we will have a nonzero number of empty squares at the end of each row of the Young diagram of \(\lambda\text{.}\) If \(m'=m\) and \(n'-n\) is the multiplicity of the largest part of \(\lambda\text{,}\) they will have the same number of parts. Otherwise, their numbers of parts will differ.
