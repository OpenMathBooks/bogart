\(f(n)=3\cdot2^n\text{.}\) We prove our formula is correct by induction. When \(n=0\) our formula gives \(f(0)=3\text{,}\) which is what we were given. Now suppose that when \(n=k-1\text{,}\) \(f(n) =3\cdot2^n\text{.}\) Then \(f(k)=2\cdot f(k-1) =2\cdot 3\cdot2^{k-1}=3\cdot2^k\text{.}\) Therefore the truth of our formula when \(n=k-1\) implies its truth when \(n=k\) and so by the principle of mathematical induction, \(f(n)=3\cdot 2^n\) for all nonnegative integers \(n\text{.}\)
