\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty B_{n+1}\frac{x^n}{n!} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty\binom{n}{k}B_{n-k}\frac{x^n}{n!} =\sum_{i=0}^\infty B_{i}\frac{x^{i}}{(i)!}\sum_{j=0}^\infty \frac{x^j}{j!} \text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus \(B'(x) = B(x)e^x\text{,}\) which gives us \(\ln B(x) = e^x+c\text{,}\) or \(B(x) = e^{ex+c} =e^ce^{(e^x)}\text{.}\) Since \(B_0=B(0)\) and \(B_0=1\text{,}\) we have \(c=-1\) and \(B(x) = \exp(e^x-1)\text{.}\)
