If a vertex \(w\) is connected by a walk to a vertex \(v\) in \(C\text{,}\) then it is connected to \(x\) by the walk that goes from \(w\) to \(v\) and then from \(v\) to \(x\text{.}\) Thus no vertex \(w\) in the graph other than a member of \(C\) is connected by a walk to any vertex in \(C\text{.}\)
